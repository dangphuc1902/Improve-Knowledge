# SESSION 08 — Handling Difficult Questions

> **SRS Level**: Advanced | **Review**: Every 7 days
> **Goal**: Never freeze, never say "I don't know", recover gracefully

---

## 🃏 CARD 1 — You Don't Know the Answer

**Situation**: Interviewer asks about something you haven't used.

❌ Don't say:
- "I don't know."
- "I never learned that."
- "We didn't use that in my company."

✅ Recovery Strategy 1 — Bridge to What You Know:
> "I haven't worked directly with [X], but it's on my radar. From what I understand, it [describe high-level concept]. In my current work, I've achieved something similar using [what you DO know] — would it be helpful if I walk you through that approach?"

✅ Recovery Strategy 2 — Show Analytical Thinking:
> "That's not something I've implemented hands-on yet. But let me think through it based on what I know about the problem it solves... [think out loud]. Is my understanding roughly in the right direction? I'd love to discuss the nuances."

✅ Recovery Strategy 3 — Honest + Curious:
> "Honestly, that specific scenario hasn't come up in my work yet. But I'm very interested — could you share a bit about how your team has approached it? That context would help me understand if there are gaps I should address."

**🔑 Key phrases:**
- *"it's on my radar"* = tôi có biết đến nó
- *"walk you through"* = giải thích từng bước
- *"think out loud"* = suy nghĩ to (để interviewer thấy quá trình)
- *"gaps I should address"* = những thiếu hụt tôi cần bổ sung

## 🃏 CARD 2 — You Made an Error in Your Answer

**Situation**: You realize mid-answer that you said something wrong.

❌ Don't: Panic, go silent, or keep going hoping they didn't notice

✅ Graceful correction:
> "Actually, let me correct myself on that — I said [X], but that's not quite right. What I meant to say is [correct answer]. I conflated it with [similar concept]. Thanks for letting me think through that."

> "Hold on, I want to revisit what I just said. I stated that [X], but actually on reflection, the correct behavior is [Y]. That was a slip — let me be more precise."

**🔑 Key phrases:**
- *"let me correct myself"* = để tôi tự sửa lại
- *"not quite right"* = không hoàn toàn đúng
- *"I conflated it with"* = tôi nhầm lẫn với
- *"on reflection"* = khi suy nghĩ lại
- *"let me be more precise"* = để tôi nói chính xác hơn

**📝 YOUR SCORE: [ ]✅ [ ]⚠️ [ ]❌**

## 🃏 CARD 3 — Question is Too Vague / You Need Clarification

**Situation**: The question is ambiguous or you're not sure what they're really asking.

✅ Asking for clarification naturally:
> "That's a broad area — could you help me narrow down the focus? Are you asking more about [interpretation A] or [interpretation B]?"

> "Just to make sure I answer what you're looking for — are you interested in the theoretical concept, or a practical example from production?"

> "When you say [term], do you mean [meaning A] or [meaning B]? They're related but I want to make sure I'm addressing what's most relevant."

> "Could you give me a bit more context? Is this in the context of a high-traffic system, or a standard enterprise application? My answer might be slightly different."

**🔑 Key phrases:**
- *"narrow down the focus"* = thu hẹp phạm vi
- *"just to make sure"* = chỉ để chắc chắn
- *"practical example from production"* = ví dụ thực tế từ môi trường production
- *"could you give me more context"* = bạn có thể cho thêm ngữ cảnh không

**📝 YOUR SCORE: [ ]✅ [ ]⚠️ [ ]❌**

## 🃏 CARD 4 — Thinking Out Loud (System Design)

**Situation**: Given a system design question. You need to think through it.

✅ How to structure your thinking aloud:
> "Let me approach this systematically. First, I'd want to clarify the requirements — both functional and non-functional. [Ask 2-3 clarifying questions.]

> Okay, so given that we need to handle [X scale] with [Y constraint], let me start from a high level and drill down.

> At the highest level, I'd structure this as... [describe architecture]. 

> The main challenges I see are: [list 2-3 challenges]. Let me tackle each one.

> For [Challenge 1]... I'd use [solution] because [reason]. The trade-off is [pro vs con].

> I'll pause there — does this direction make sense to you, or is there a specific area you'd like me to go deeper on?"

**🔑 Key phrases:**
- *"approach this systematically"* = tiếp cận có hệ thống
- *"drill down"* = đi sâu vào chi tiết
- *"I pause there"* = tôi dừng ở đây
- *"trade-off"* = đánh đổi
- *"does this direction make sense"* = hướng này có hợp lý không

**📝 YOUR SCORE: [ ]✅ [ ]⚠️ [ ]❌**

## 🃏 CARD 5 — Salary Negotiation in English

**Situation**: "What are your salary expectations?"

✅ Professional approach:
> "Based on my research on the current market for Java Backend Engineers with my experience level in [city] — and considering the scope of this role — I'm targeting somewhere in the range of [X] to [Y] per month.

> That said, I'm open to a conversation. Compensation for me is about the full picture — the base, the growth opportunity, and the team culture. If there's flexibility on the base, I'm happy to discuss what the total package looks like.

> Could you share what the budgeted range is for this position? That way I can share whether we're in the same ballpark."

**🔑 Key phrases:**
- *"total package"* = tổng gói đãi ngộ
- *"in the same ballpark"* = trong cùng một phạm vi
- *"flexibility"* = sự linh hoạt
- *"I'm open to a conversation"* = tôi sẵn sàng thương lượng

**📝 YOUR SCORE: [ ]✅ [ ]⚠️ [ ]❌**

## 🃏 CARD 6 — Closing Strong

**Situation**: "Do you have any questions for us?"

✅ NEVER say "No, I think I'm good."

✅ Strong questions to ask:
1. *"What does success look like for someone in this role in the first 90 days?"*
2. *"What's the biggest technical challenge the team is currently facing?"*
3. *"How does the team handle technical debt — is there dedicated time for it?"*
4. *"What's the culture like around learning and professional development?"*
5. *"What do you personally enjoy most about working here?"* (for rapport)

**After asking**, respond to their answer naturally:
> "That's really helpful context, thank you. It sounds like [summarize what they said] — that's exactly the kind of [challenge/environment/team] I'm looking for."

## 🎤 Emergency Phrases (Memorize These)

| Situation | Phrase |
|---|---|
| Need more time | *"Let me think about that for a moment..."* |
| Didn't understand | *"I'm sorry, could you rephrase that?"* |
| Made a mistake | *"Actually, let me correct myself on that..."* |
| Don't know | *"That's not my strongest area, but here's how I'd reason through it..."* |
| Want to redirect | *"That's interesting — a related thing I've dealt with is..."* |
| Need to wrap up | *"To summarize my point..."* |
| Gaining buy-in | *"Does that answer your question, or would you like me to go deeper?"* |
