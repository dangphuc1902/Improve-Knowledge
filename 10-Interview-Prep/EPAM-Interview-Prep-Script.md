## Part 4: English Proficiency — Preparation

The interviewer will assess your English **throughout the conversation**, not in a separate test. Here's how to prepare:

### ✅ Do's

| Technique | Example |
|:---|:---|
| **Speak in complete sentences** | "I optimized the query performance by 40%." NOT "Query... optimize... 40%." |
| **Use transition phrases** | "First... Second... Finally...", "In addition to that...", "On top of that..." |
| **Quantify achievements** | "Reduced latency by 30%", "Supported 5,000 concurrent users" |
| **Use professional vocabulary** | "Architected", "Implemented", "Mentored", "Optimized", "Standardized" |
| **Pause and structure** | "That's a great question. Let me structure my answer..." |

### ❌ Don'ts

| Mistake | Better Alternative |
|:---|:---|
| "I did a project..." | "I **architected and implemented** a 10-service microservices system..." |
| "It's very fast..." | "The system was **stress-tested to support 10,000 concurrent users**..." |
| "I don't know" | "I haven't had the opportunity to use that in production, but **based on my understanding**..." |
| Long silence | "Let me take a moment to organize my thoughts on this..." |
| "Sorry my English bad" | Never apologize — just speak clearly and confidently |

### 🛠️ Emergency Phrases

**When you don't understand:**
- "Could you please rephrase the question? I want to make sure I fully understand."
- "Just to clarify — are you asking about [A] or [B]?"

**When you need time:**
- "That's an interesting question. Let me take a moment to structure my thoughts."
- "Let me think about that for a second..."

**When you make a mistake:**
- "Let me correct myself. What I meant to say was..."
- "Actually, I'd like to clarify my previous point..."

**When you don't know something:**
- "I haven't used that specific technology in production yet, but based on my knowledge of [related concept], I would approach it like this..."
