# PART 2 - ENGLISH COMMUNICATION ROUND

> **Goal**: Demonstrate professional English for technical roles in enterprise/outsourcing environments

---

## Q1. Can you introduce yourself in English?

### ✅ Sample Answer
> "Good morning! I'm Phuc, a Java Backend Engineer with around 3 years of experience. I specialize in building enterprise applications using Spring Boot, Hibernate, and Oracle. I've worked on projects in the fintech and telecom domains.
>
> In my current role, I collaborate daily with cross-functional teams — including business analysts, QA engineers, and project managers — and I also communicate with overseas clients on requirement clarification and status updates.
>
> I'm comfortable working in English environments and enjoy technical discussions. I look forward to this conversation."

### 📚 Key Vocabulary
| Word/Phrase | Meaning | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| specialize in | chuyên về | "I specialize in backend development" |
| cross-functional | liên bộ phận | "cross-functional team" |
| collaborate | hợp tác | "I collaborate with PMs daily" |
| clarification | làm rõ | "requirement clarification" |
| status update | cập nhật tiến độ | "daily status update" |

### 💎 Better Version (Native-like)
> "Hey, great to meet you! I'm Phuc — backend developer, Java enthusiast, roughly 3 years in the industry. I've been heads-down on enterprise projects — mainly fintech and telecom — building scalable APIs, wrangling microservices, and keeping databases happy. I'm the kind of engineer who takes ownership end-to-end, from design to deployment. Excited to chat today."

### 🗣️ Native-like Expressions
- *"heads-down on"* = tập trung làm việc chăm chỉ vào
- *"wrangling"* = xử lý/giải quyết (informal, technical)
- *"take ownership"* = chịu trách nhiệm hoàn toàn
- *"end-to-end"* = từ đầu đến cuối

## Q2. How do you communicate a technical problem to a non-technical client?

### ✅ Sample Answer
> "When I need to explain a technical issue to a non-technical client, I avoid jargon and use analogies they can relate to.
>
> For example, once we had a database deadlock issue causing transaction failures. Instead of explaining database locking mechanisms, I told the client: 'Imagine two people trying to enter through the same door at the same time — neither can get through. Our system had two processes doing the same thing simultaneously. We've fixed it by setting a priority so they take turns.'
>
> I also use visuals — a simple diagram often communicates more than paragraphs of text. And I always focus on the business impact and resolution timeline rather than technical details."

### 📚 Key Vocabulary
| Word/Phrase | Meaning |
|---|---|
| avoid jargon | tránh thuật ngữ kỹ thuật |
| analogy | phép ẩn dụ/so sánh |
| business impact | tác động đến nghiệp vụ |
| resolution timeline | thời gian giải quyết |
| simultaneously | đồng thời |

### 💎 Better Version
> "I translate tech into business language. The client cares about 'what does this mean for me?' — not how it works under the hood. I keep it impact-first: 'This caused X users to experience Y problem. We identified the root cause and expect full resolution by Z.' I back it up with a simple visual if needed."

### 🗣️ Native-like Expressions
- *"under the hood"* = phía sau (kỹ thuật bên trong)
- *"impact-first"* = ưu tiên nói tác động trước
- *"root cause"* = nguyên nhân gốc rễ

## Q3. How do you participate in a daily standup meeting?

### ✅ Sample Answer
> "I follow the standard three-part format for standups:
>
> First: What I did yesterday — for example, 'Yesterday I completed the user authentication API and submitted it for code review.'
>
> Second: What I'm doing today — 'Today I'll be working on the payment service integration and writing unit tests.'
>
> Third: Any blockers — 'I'm waiting for the database schema update from the DB team. I'll follow up with them after this meeting.'
>
> I keep my updates concise — usually under 60 seconds — so the meeting stays efficient for the whole team."

### 📚 Key Vocabulary
| Word/Phrase | Meaning |
|---|---|
| standup | cuộc họp đứng nhanh hằng ngày |
| blocker | vật cản, điều đang chặn tiến độ |
| concise | ngắn gọn, súc tích |
| code review | review code |
| follow up | theo dõi, tiếp tục liên hệ |

### 💎 Better Version
> "Done yesterday: finished auth API, pushed to review. Today: payment integration. Blockers: DB schema — pinging the DB team right after this."

### 🗣️ Native-like Expressions
- *"pinging"* = nhắn tin/liên hệ nhanh (informal)
- *"pushed to review"* = đã đưa lên để review

## Q4. How do you report project status to a foreign client?

### ✅ Sample Answer
> "When reporting to a foreign client, I usually prepare a brief written summary along with a verbal update.
>
> I structure it as: Progress this week, Upcoming tasks, Risks or concerns, and Questions or decisions needed.
>
> For example, I'd say: 'This week we completed the API integration with your payment gateway and deployed it to the staging environment. Testing is in progress and we're targeting UAT by next Friday. One risk I want to flag: the third-party API documentation has some gaps — could you connect us with their technical team?'
>
> I always write clear meeting notes afterward and share them via email so there's a written record."

### 📚 Key Vocabulary
| Word/Phrase | Meaning |
|---|---|
| UAT (User Acceptance Testing) | kiểm thử chấp nhận của người dùng |
| staging environment | môi trường kiểm thử trước production |
| flag a risk | cảnh báo rủi ro |
| written record | hồ sơ/tài liệu bằng văn bản |
| meeting notes | biên bản cuộc họp |

### 💎 Better Version
> "Quick status: we hit our milestone this week — payment gateway integration is live in staging. UAT kicks off Monday, targeting sign-off by Friday. One flag: their API docs are incomplete — I've drafted a question list to send over. No blockers on our end."

### 🗣️ Native-like Expressions
- *"hit our milestone"* = đạt được mốc quan trọng
- *"kicks off"* = bắt đầu
- *"sign-off"* = phê duyệt chính thức
- *"on our end"* = về phía chúng tôi

## Q5. How do you handle a situation where you don't understand a client's requirement?

### ✅ Sample Answer
> "Honestly, this happens and it's completely normal. My approach is to never pretend I understand when I don't — that leads to wasted effort and miscommunication.
>
> I ask clarifying questions immediately: 'Could you give me an example of how you'd expect this to work?' or 'Just to confirm my understanding — are you saying that X should happen when Y occurs?'
>
> I also use the technique of restating the requirement back: 'Let me make sure I've got this right. You want the system to...' — this validates understanding without making the client feel I wasn't listening.
>
> If the requirement is complex, I follow up with a written summary via email or a simple diagram to get confirmation before development starts."

### 📚 Key Vocabulary
| Word/Phrase | Meaning |
|---|---|
| clarifying questions | câu hỏi làm rõ |
| miscommunication | giao tiếp sai/hiểu nhầm |
| validate | xác nhận |
| restating | diễn đạt lại |
| confirmation | xác nhận |

### 💎 Better Version
> "I never assume. If something's unclear, I stop and ask right there — 'Can you walk me through a use case?' or 'Let me play that back to you...' It's faster to spend 2 minutes clarifying than 2 weeks building the wrong thing."

### 🗣️ Native-like Expressions
- *"play that back to you"* = diễn đạt lại để xác nhận
- *"walk me through"* = giải thích từng bước cho tôi
- *"never assume"* = không bao giờ giả định

## Q6. Describe how you handle a production incident in English.

### ✅ Sample Answer
> "When a production incident happens, I follow a structured approach.
>
> First, I acknowledge the issue immediately and communicate to stakeholders: 'We're aware of the issue and investigating. We'll have an update in 30 minutes.'
>
> Then I start diagnosing — checking logs in Kibana, reviewing recent deployments, and isolating the affected service.
>
> Once I identify the root cause, I implement a fix — sometimes a hotfix, sometimes a rollback — and test it in staging before pushing to production.
>
> After resolution, I write a post-mortem: what happened, why it happened, how we fixed it, and what we're doing to prevent it from recurring.
>
> Clear communication throughout is critical — never go silent during an incident."

### 📚 Key Vocabulary
| Word/Phrase | Meaning |
|---|---|
| production incident | sự cố trên môi trường thật |
| stakeholders | các bên liên quan |
| diagnosing | chẩn đoán |
| hotfix | bản vá nhanh |
| post-mortem | báo cáo phân tích sau sự cố |
| rollback | khôi phục phiên bản trước |
| recurring | tái diễn |

### 💎 Better Version
> "Triage first: confirm the scope, communicate an ETA for update, then go heads-down on root cause. I don't fix blind — I look at logs, diff recent deploys, isolate the blast radius. Once fixed and verified in staging, push to prod and send a clear all-clear message. Post-mortem within 24 hours."

### 🗣️ Native-like Expressions
- *"triage"* = phân loại độ nghiêm trọng
- *"ETA"* = Estimated Time of Arrival (thời gian dự kiến)
- *"blast radius"* = phạm vi ảnh hưởng
- *"all-clear"* = thông báo vấn đề đã được giải quyết
