## 🗣️ Common Interview Phrases

### When Clarifying Requirements
| Situation | Phrase |
|:----------|:-------|
| Need more info | "Could you clarify what you mean by...?" |
| Confirm understanding | "So if I understand correctly, you'd like me to..." |
| Ask about constraints | "What are the expected scale and performance requirements?" |
| Narrow scope | "For the scope of this discussion, should I focus on...?" |

### When Explaining Your Approach
| Situation | Phrase |
|:----------|:-------|
| Starting | "Let me walk you through my approach..." |
| Trade-off | "The trade-off here is between [A] and [B]. I chose [A] because..." |
| Alternative | "An alternative approach would be..., but I prefer this because..." |
| Complexity | "The time complexity of this solution is O(n), and space is O(1)..." |

### When Discussing Past Experience
| Situation | Phrase |
|:----------|:-------|
| Setting context | "In my previous role at [company], I was responsible for..." |
| Describing impact | "This resulted in a [X]% improvement in [metric]..." |
| Showing learning | "One key takeaway from that experience was..." |
| Ownership | "I took the initiative to..." / "I led the effort to..." |

### When You're Stuck
| Situation | Phrase |
|:----------|:-------|
| Need time | "That's a great question. Let me think about that for a moment..." |
| Partial answer | "I'm not 100% sure about [specific detail], but my understanding is..." |
| Ask for hint | "Could you give me a hint on which direction to explore?" |
| Redirect | "I haven't worked with [X] specifically, but in a similar situation with [Y], I..." |

### When Asking Questions
| Situation | Phrase |
|:----------|:-------|
| Tech stack | "What does the current tech stack look like for this team?" |
| Team structure | "How is the engineering team organized? Do you use a squad model?" |
| Growth | "What does career growth look like for this role?" |
| Culture | "How does the team approach code reviews and knowledge sharing?" |
| Product | "What are the biggest technical challenges the team is currently facing?" |

## 📅 Daily English Practice Routine (30 min)

### Monday: Shadowing
1. Pick 1 tech YouTube video (5-10 min) — Fireship, TechLead, ByteByteGo
2. Watch once with subtitles
3. Replay and speak along (shadow) — focus on pronunciation and rhythm
4. Note 5 new words/phrases

### Tuesday: Reading
1. Read 1 tech article on Medium, Dev.to, or company tech blog
2. Highlight unknown words → add to vocabulary list
3. Write 3-sentence summary in English

### Wednesday: Listening
1. Listen to "Software Engineering Daily" or "The Changelog" (15 min)
2. Note main topics discussed
3. Try to explain what you heard (to yourself)

### Thursday: Writing
1. Write about a technical topic in English (200-300 words)
2. Topics: describe your project, explain a design decision, write a tech blog draft
3. Use Grammarly or similar to check

### Friday: Speaking
1. Record yourself answering 1 interview question (2 min)
2. Listen back — check: clarity, filler words ("um", "uh"), confidence
3. Re-record if needed

### Saturday: Extended Session
1. Watch a full System Design video in English (30-45 min)
2. Take notes in English
3. Try to explain the design back in English

### Sunday: Review & Write
1. Review vocabulary from the week
2. Write a short blog post or LinkedIn update in English
3. Relax — watch a tech talk for fun

## 💡 Common Mistakes Vietnamese Developers Make in English Interviews

| Mistake | Fix |
|:--------|:----|
| Speaking too fast when nervous | Slow down. Pause between sentences. |
| Using "we" instead of "I" | Interviewers want to know YOUR contribution. Use "I". |
| Not asking clarifying questions | Always ask 2-3 questions before diving into a solution. |
| Saying "I don't know" and stopping | Say "I'm not sure, but here's how I'd approach it..." |
| Translating literally from Vietnamese | Learn English tech idioms: "under the hood", "out of the box", etc. |
| Filler words: "actually", "basically" | Practice pausing instead of filling silence. |
| Not quantifying results | Always include numbers: "reduced by 30%", "5000 users" |
